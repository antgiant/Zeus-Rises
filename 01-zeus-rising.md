# Zeus Rising

The night that would forever change Jane’s life began like countless other nights before it. It was Thursday and clear so Jane set up her telescopes on the top of Newton Parking garage. Just like she did every clear Thursday night for anyone interested in looking at the sky but especially for her Astronomy students. Monday had been a new moon so there was less distraction from the moon than normal, and the Geminids meteor shower would peak next Monday so there was decent meteorite viewing as well.

Sadly, only John had shown up and as usual he spent the night stubbornly trying to prove his astrology theories. Jane consoled herself that at least she had finally gotten through to him enough that he was now making testable predictions about the future. She hoped that meant it was only a matter of time before he finally saw the light. When he finally wrapped up and headed out for the night, and Jane decided she had just enough time take a real swing at a tricky bit of photography she had been wanting to do for a while. 

Jane had been wanting to photograph Jupiter with one of its moons rising over the city skyline, and now she had just enough time to setup and catch it with Jupiter’s moon Io appearing almost directly underneath Jupiter. As a bonus tonight she could also capture the rising Leo constellation with Mars at the same time. So she got to work. First she setup her phone on a tripod and aimed to capture the skyline and rising Leo. Then she setup her ancient DLSR camera on a second motorized tripod and setup the tripod to track Mars, but offset so that it captured both Mars and Jupiter. Finally she setup her big 10 inch scope tightly zoomed in on Jupiter. She had barely completed setup when Io started to rise (sink?). Resulting in a panicked 10 minutes of bouncing between the three devices ensuring that they were capturing it all. Then as quickly as it began it was over, Jane packed up her gear and went home. 

When she got home Jane couldn’t contain herself and created a quick composite zoom for her Twitter feed before heading to bed. As, she was quickly tossing together the triple zoom image she noticed that there was an artifact in the 10 inch images that almost looked like another planet rising behind the opposite side of Jupiter of Io. She checked the other images from the 10 inch and they all had it too. Odd. However, Jane was tired and it was late. So, rather than investigate further she just added a joke label about Zeus rising and hit post. Then she went to bed.
