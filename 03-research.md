# Chapter 3 - Research

It is a common misconception that the job of University Faculty is to teach. That is merely the cover story for their real job which is to earn grants. More specifically the job of faculty is to earn grants for their research and ideally enough fame and prestige that others can ride their coat tails into grants as well. Jane’s Astronomy research grant was nearly depleted, so above all else her job was dependent on her completing grant applications to keep up her research funded. As a lowly assistant professor Jane also had more than a few classes to teach and some advisees as well.  For extra motivation, Jane’s contract had made crystal It clear that her position was entirely funded with grant dollars. So, if she wanted to avoid being relegated back to the respect & research free purgatory that is adjunct teaching a new grant was vital.

However, today was special. Jane had finally completed all the grant application paperwork! She finally has a few previous hours to do some analysis of the atmospheric anomaly she has found on her new favorite exoplanet! She unlocked her computer and excitedly launched her favorite analysis toolset. While she waited for it to load, she switched over to her email app for a quick look. Just to make sure none of her advisees had any new, actually urgent, crisis. That was when Jane discovered once again that she would not be performing her analysis today. Instead, she would, yet again, be doing grant paperwork. Nothing quite like the joy of spending an afternoon detailing rationales for everything in a grant application right down to each $5 app and $10 USB drive to crush the spirit.
