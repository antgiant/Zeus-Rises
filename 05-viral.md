# Chapter 5 - Viral 

While Jane enjoyed her usual overly sound sleep, she slept right through her cell phone trying with increasing vigor to commit suicide by buzzing itself off of the nightstand to the tile below. When it finally succeeded in making the leap the charging cable made a valiant effort to stop the fall but ultimately failed, and Jane didn’t even reward it with a flinch. So, the phone vibrated its way into the deepest darkest corner it could find, conveniently under a long-forgotten pillow, and quietly vibrated its battery into oblivion. Which was why Jane found herself starting awake in a panic several hours later. That special kind of panic where you know something is wrong and you are instantly fully awake. It took a moment but then it hit her. Christmas music! Obnoxiously loud, odiously religious, Christmas music. That could only be the store two streets over, but they don’t open till 9. An hour after she was due at work.

On no! 

Her alarm has failed her!

She immediately grabbed her phone to see what time it was. Only to discover her phone was missing too! Jane launched herself out of bed and into her morning routine. In record time she was scrambling out the door and off to work extra proud of herself for even finding her dead cell phone under the first pillow she checked.

As she careened towards the University, she briefly saw the 9:42 on the old bank sign. That meant she was going to be at least two hours late. As she flew down the street dodging the stray overly spandexed bicyclist she started figuring the calculus on consequences for today’s oversleep. It was Friday so she only had office hours till 10. No one had scheduled a meeting as of last night and her first class didn’t start till 10:15. She might survive this after all! 

She found a parking spot at 10:09 and burst from the car, sprinting to her office to collect supplies. She was relieved to see no one waiting outside her door as she flew down the hallway. She grabbed her supplies, plugged in her phone, and was gone, so fast she was halfway to class before it hit her that there was something strange about her office. Why was there a light on her desk? Maybe her phone had a voicemail? Maybe it wasn’t broken after all? No time to worry about it now. Here was her class. Jane paused a moment to compose herself and then stepped through the door exactly at 10:15.

An hour and a half later Jane finished answering questions on Goldilocks zone exoplanets and headed back to her office to collect her things for her noon Astronomy 101 class. When she opened her office door the first thing she noticed was her phone going nuts with notifications. Jane didn’t even know it was possible for notifications to come in so fast. After watching in awe for a few moments she tapped on a notification which promptly took her to her social media app. There to her surprise she got an entirely new screen.

## Redo to follow cycle (Viral Discovery during research time)

“Congratulations! You have gone viral! We recognize that this exciting time can also be overwhelming. Here are some tools to help you.”

The following few screens helped Jane quickly reduce her notifications to a reasonable level while showing her a whole new world of tools for managing her social media feed. Jane struggled to follow along. Finally, she made it to the end and into her feed, where her last post was flagged as having gone massively viral. Confused, Jane clicked on the post to see what the fuss was about. What she found confused her even more. The comments were focused on something called Zeus. Jane rechecked the image and was reminded that she had added a joke label about that. Now it seemed that people thought that was a real planet. Why was everyone so worked up about this artifact in her image?

## Change this to the next day during cycle

Just then her boss and the department chair Jerold exploded through the door.

“Where is your next class?” He shouted at her.

Confused and more than a bit uncomfortable Jane replied. “My Astronomy 101 class is over at Copernicus hall in … 5 minutes.”

“What are you covering today?”



“I’ll cover 101 myself. Just get to your office now!” Jerold shouted 

“What is going?” on Jane asked in bewilderment 

“Just go!” He shouted

Jane fumbled her things together as fast as she could under the disapproving eye of Jerold, and scrambled towards her office.

As she scrambled across campus Jane grabbed her phone to see if there were emails or messages she had missed. Only to be reminded that her phone was still dead. So, she nervously wracked her brain as she rushed.
Did I miss a grant deadline? 
No I filed before last week’s deadline and I still have a week and a half before the next deadline.
Maybe a student complained?
About what?
Must be grant related! What else could be this urgent? 
What could I have messed up badly enough for this urgency?
Oh no!
Did I forget to include the budget summary on SF-424?
No I distinctly remember doing that.

She burst into the building and immediately noticed Jerry standing outside her office door. Jerry?!? Why would the nauseating, condescending, stud muffin from marketing be outside her door?

“Good afternoon Jerry” Jane puffed, wishing she was a little less out of breath.

“There you are.” said Jerry with his trademark grinch smile and feigned interest. “I guess you are not dead after all, that’s a relief.”

“Wha…” Jane started to reply 

“You need to come with me to the studio right now!” his smile somehow thinning even further at the “interruption”. “You break the story of the century and then go completely off grid! You cannot begin to imagine headaches you have caused me today! Five articles claiming you have been abducted by the government, and two claiming you are an alien in the past three hours alone! We had to call a press conference just to prove you were still alive and a real faculty member! Thank god Jerold was able to find you and get you here!  
Pick up the pace, those reporters are about to revolt!”

Jerry burst out the door of the building very nearly turning its wreath into a festive frisbee with the force he applied.

Jerry continued to rant at Jane as he rushed through campus but Jane was no longer processing anything he said. In her utterly befuddled state it was all she could do to keep up and upright. She has not even the slightest idea what Jerry was talking about. Her last paper on the atmospheric composition of TOI-2977 b was good work but not the kind reporters would ever care about. Her current paper on MOA-2007-BLG-400L b was good science but equally unlikely to fill a room with reporters. So while most of Jane’s mind fruitlessly searched for answers the remainder she focused on keeping her feet sorted and underneath her.

As they rushed up to the Ibsen Center Jerry went silent waiting for Jane’s answer. It took Jane half a dozen steps to even notice. When she finally did she half-heartedly asked “What?”

Jerry glared at her. “Go to the green room and get ready.” He said waving vaguely down the hall before caressing his hair into place in a nearby reflection and grandly entering the performance hall backstage door.

Jane looked blankly down the hall. There were no green doors. There were Pink door, yellow door, rainbow door, and even a horrific magenta and red striped door, but nothing remotely green.  Having no idea what else to do Jane started down the hall looking into each room to see if maybe the inside walls are green. Janitorial closet with unpainted walls, nope. Lights, hooks, ropes with yellow walls, nope. Costumes, endless rows of costumes with rainbow walls, nope. Some sort of empty dressing room walls, nope. 

Jane was stumped there were no more doors. She hesitatingly started back for the rust colored door Jerry had gone through. At least someone is there who can tell me where this fabled green room is she thought. She was about halfway there when she heard 

“Dr. Jane?”

From behind her. She turned around to see a pleasant looking woman peaking out of that horrid magenta and red striped door.

“Yes?” Jane replied with confusion.

“Oh good! Come! Come! You need to get ready first.” She said beckoning with urgency. 

“What’s going on?” Jane asked with confusion as she started towards the woman. “Everyone is in such a hurry but I still have no idea what is going on.”

“I’m sorry Jane.” The woman replied.”I just do makeup. All I know there are a lot of excited people here to talk to you. Something about the stars….that’s all I know.” 

“My makeup?” Jane asked 

“Oh yes we always touch up makeup before sending people on TV.” She replied.

“A room full of people? On TV? Makeup?” Jane mumbled as she collapsed into the chair the makeup artist was gesturing to.

The next few minutes of mindless small talk and facial contortions were just what Jane needed to calm down, collect her thoughts, and prepare herself. She was about to be on TV with a lot of reporters asking questions and she had no idea about what. Must be astronomy related. So, basically treat this like a study session with a group of freshman astronomy students. Expect ridiculous questions, and stay composed no matter what.

“Okay, you are all set Dr. Jane. Just go over there and break a leg.” Said the makeup artist, gesturing towards an opening Jane hadn’t noticed.

Jane walked over to the archway and just on the other side was a bored looking drama student. 

“You must be Dr. Jane” he said 

“What’s going on here?” Asked Jane

“Jerry didn’t tell you? Let me guess he just monologued the whole time didn’t he?”

Jane nodded.

“That guy is such a pompous jerk! Nothing interesting here. Just some boring press conference about that new planet you discovered. Hercules or something. The stuffed suits trashed our Les Miz dress rehearsal for it. You're up. Go break a leg.” He said pointing her towards the podium.

Suddenly, Jane found herself walking across the stage to the podium with a roomful of eyes and cameras watching her. When she arrived at the podium the person standing there said.

“Dr. Johnson, I know you just came straight from teaching a class. Do you have a prepared statement?”

Jane shook her head no, while silently wishing she had any idea what was going on.

“Ok then straight to questions. Who has a question for Dr. Johnson?”

In the cacophony that followed Jane found herself facing a barrage of questions. 

“How could a new planet suddenly appear?”

“New planets are being found all the time.”

“Is there alien life on this new planet?

“Detecting signs of life on another planet is very difficult with our current tools. Even if there is we won’t know quickly.”

“Are you an alien?”

“No”

“Any comment on why so far only earth-based observatories can confirm your discovery?”

“Time on telescopes is hard to come by, much more so for space-based telescopes. I am not aware of any that have tried to confirm my work.”

Finally, after an eternal half an hour the questions started to peter out and the University PR person stepped up to the microphone.

“Thank you all for coming today. Periauger University is proud to have eminent scholars like Dr. Johnson and a well-rounded collection of degrees to transport all young scholars to the heights of learning. That is all for today.”

He then escorted Jane off the stage.
 
Jane went back towards her office still trying to make sense of what had just happened. Apparently, lots of people thought she had discovered a brand new planet, but why? As she walked Jane smiled at the ludicrousness of being asked if she was an alien, and worried about what additional surprises her email and other messages held for her. Then with a little start of surprise Jane discovered that she was standing in front of her favorite old hot dog stand and was feeling quite hungry. Realizing that she hadn’t eaten all day and it smelled delicious, she decided it was lunch time. A few minutes later as she continued on to her office it occurred to her to wonder who had seen her press conference. 

Finally at her office, Jane hung up her do not disturb sign and put her phone on its charger and sat down to enjoy her hot dog in peace. Her phone had other plans. She was only a bite or two into her hot dog when her phone finished auto turning on and promptly started buzzing.

## Move back to day earlier
Then she turned on her laptop. As soon as she logged in the tsunami of alerts and notifications began. With a nervous sigh she started to wade through them. 
 What she found was more diverse than she expected. There were congratulations from colleagues, homework questions from students, requests for interviews, sponsorship “opportunities”, nitpicky complaints from her peer reviewers on her most recent paper, there were even a few grant offers.

 Jane excitedly started reviewing the grant opportunities right away and was quickly disappointed to discover that they were all from crackpots. Little Green Men, LLC wanted to fund her efforts to prove that aliens were real, the Astrological Society of the Milky Way wanted her input on to improve their astrological predictions, and it went downhill from there. She couldn't help but wonder if John was was somehow behind the Astrological Society of the Milky Way. 

Needing a break from such miserable prospects Jane finally picked up her phone to see what her voicemail said. There were messages of congratulations, and a few of jealousy, there was a reminder from the PR department that she had to clear all public statements through them, and a message from her mom complaining that she never called. Messages from a few friends worried that she was dead because they hadn't been able to reach her on her cell all day. Oh, and there were messages from pretty much every local news station wanting to interview her, a few national ones too. At the very end there was an automated system message that her external phone number had been temporarily disabled due to suspected suspicious activity on the line

Jane hung up the phone in a bit of a daze taking a moment to collect her thoughts. Right then her phone rang for the first time and Jane absently answered it.

“Dr. Jane speaking”

“Dr. Johnson? Oh good, you made it to your office! I’m Julien from Marketing. Your Discovery of Zeus has made you very popular! We have been getting interview requests for you all day. I am sure you have quite a few who made it directly to you as well.”

“Sure have” Jane replied

“I thought so. Congratulations on such a momentous find! We are extremely proud to have eminent scholars like yourself here at Periauger University! I will be your personal guide to help you with this flood of requests. Is your calendar up to date, I can start booking your interviews right away. Also, I’ll need you to update your voicemail greeting to direct people to me. Oh, and if you’ll grant me access to your email I can take care of the emailed requests as well, and “

“Stop!” Jane half shouted. “Listen ‘Julien’ today has been long enough and crazy enough as is! I am not going to end it by stupidly giving some scammer access to everything!” and with that Jane slammed down the phone.

Jane shook her head at the audacity of scammers and once again tried to collect her thoughts.
