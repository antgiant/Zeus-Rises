# Viral
While Jane enjoyed her usual extra sound sleep she slept right through her cell phone trying with increasing vigor to commit suicide by buzzing itself off of the nightstand to the tile below. When it finally succeeded in making the leap the charging cable made a valiant effort to stop the fall but ultimately failed, and Jane didn’t even reward it with a flinch. So the phone vibrated its way into the deepest darkest corner it could find, conveniently under a long forgotten pillow, and quietly vibrated its battery into oblivion. Which was why Jane found herself starting awake in a panic. That special kind of panic where you know something is wrong and you are instantly fully awake. It took a moment but then it hit her. Christmas music! Obnoxiously loud, odiously religious, Christmas music. That could only be the store two streets over, but they don’t open till 9. An hour after she was due at work.

On no! Her alarm has failed her! She immediately grabbed her phone to see what time it was. Only to discover her phone was missing too! Jane launched herself out of bed and into her morning routine. In record time she was scrambling out the door and off to work extra proud of herself for even finding her dead cell phone under the first pillow she checked.

As she careened towards the University she briefly saw the 9:42 on the old bank sign. That meant she was going to be at least two hours late. As she flew down the street dodging the stray overly spandexed bicyclist she started figuring the calculus on consequences for today’s oversleep. It was Friday so she only had office hours till 10. No one had scheduled a meeting as of last night and her first class didn’t start till 10:15. She might survive this after all! 

She found a parking spot at 10:09 and burst from the car sprinting to her office to collect supplies. She was relieved to see no one waiting outside her door as she flew down the hallway. She grabbed her supplies and was gone, so fast she was halfway to class before it hit her that there was something strange about her office. Why was there a light on her desk? Maybe her phone had a voicemail? Maybe it wasn’t broken after all? No time to worry about it now. Here was her class. Jane paused a moment to compose herself and then stepped through the door exactly at 10:15.
