# Chapter 4 - Zeus Rising

Suddenly, it was December. The leaves were gone, as was last week’s light dusting of snow. However, Jane’s grant was still “in progress”, and John was still proselytizing Astrology. So, Jane continued her purgatory of a holding routine. Today being a clear Thursday meant that her jewel of the week had arrived! Jane set up her telescopes of the roof of Newton parking garage. An entire evening of stargazing was here. Anyone interested in looking at the sky was welcome, but especially for her Astronomy students. Monday had been a new moon so there was less distraction from the moon than normal, and the Geminids meteor shower would peak next Monday so there was decent meteorite viewing as well.

Sadly, only John had shown up and as usual he spent the night stubbornly “researching” his latest astrology theories. Jane consoled herself that at least she had finally gotten through to him enough that he was now at least making testable predictions about the future. She still optimistically hoped that meant it was only a matter of time before he finally saw the light. When he finally wrapped up and headed out for the night, his Jane decided she had just enough time take a real swing at a tricky bit of photography she had been wanting to do for a while. 

Jane had been wanting to photograph Jupiter with one of its moons rising over the city skyline, and now she had just enough time to setup and catch it with Jupiter’s moon Io appearing almost directly underneath Jupiter. As a bonus tonight the stars aligned, and she could also capture the rising Leo constellation with Mars at the same time. So she got to work. First, she setup her phone on a tripod and aimed to capture the skyline and rising Leo. Then she setup her ancient DLSR camera on a second motorized tripod and setup the tripod to track Mars, but offset so that it captured both Mars and Jupiter. Finally, she setup her big 10-inch scope tightly zoomed in on Jupiter. She had barely completed setup when Io started to peak out beneath Jupiter. This resulted in a panicked 10 minutes of bouncing between the three devices ensuring that they were capturing everything. Then as quickly as it began it was over. So, Jane packed up her gear and went home. 

When she got home, she couldn’t contain herself and decided to create a quick and dirty image mock-up.  As, she was tossing together the triple zoom image she noticed that there was an artifact in the 10-inch images that almost looked like another planet rising behind the side of Jupiter opposite of Io. She checked the other images from the 10-inch, and they all had it too. Odd. However, Jane was tired, and it was late. So, rather than investigate further she just added a joke label about Zeus rising and hit post on her public social media feed. Then she went to bed.
